How Does the Strategy Work?

This strategy involves trading pairs of currencies in a way that aims to neutralize
market risk, focusing on the relative performance of the paired currencies rather
than the overall market direction. Once the pairs have been tested for mean
reverting stationarity and the cointegrating nature of the pairs is confirmed, I
determine the mean point of control price. This is a significant price level
where historical trading volumes concentrate, suggesting a key level in the market.
To optimize my entries, I use a linear order sequence. This sequence helps me
systematically execute trades, averaging my entry prices away from the historical
mean price. This approach allows for precise entry points based on statistical analysis.
If the average entry price moves beyond a predefined threshold within an expiration
cycle, I balance the positions by using an equal hedge. This step ensures risk
management by maintaining a balanced exposure in the market.
